Privacy Policy

My BP Diary — 我的血壓日記

Independent Developer  ·  Effective Date: March 29, 2026

✅ Our Core Privacy Commitment

  • We never collect, access, or transmit your health or medical data
  • All physiological data is stored exclusively on your device and your iCloud account
  • The developer is technically incapable of accessing any of your personal information

1Introduction

My BP Diary (also known as 我的血壓日記) is developed and maintained by an independent individual developer. This application is designed to help you conveniently record and track blood pressure measurements for personal health awareness.

This Privacy Policy explains how we treat your information — and more importantly, how we do not collect it. Please read this document carefully to understand our practices regarding any information related to your use of this application.

2Information We Collect

📌 My BP Diary does not collect, store on remote servers, or transmit any personal health data. All blood pressure readings, pulse rates, dates, and notes you enter remain entirely on your device. The developer has no access to this information at any time.

This application does not use any of the following:

  • Advertising tracking or behavioral analytics (e.g., Meta Pixel, Google Analytics)
  • Crash reporting services that upload user data (e.g., Firebase Crashlytics)
  • Third-party data collection or telemetry SDKs
  • Session recording, heatmaps, or interaction tracking tools

3Health & Physiological Data

My BP Diary handles sensitive health information. We are committed to the following practices in full compliance with Apple App Store Review Guidelines and applicable privacy regulations:

  • Health data (blood pressure, pulse, timestamps, and associated notes) is never collected by the developer
  • Health data is never used for advertising, marketing, or sale to third parties
  • Health data is never disclosed to third parties for any purpose other than as required by law
  • Health data is processed solely on your device or via Apple's encrypted CloudKit infrastructure

4How Data Is Stored

💾 Local Storage — Apple SwiftData

All blood pressure and pulse readings you enter are stored locally on your device using Apple SwiftData. This data never leaves your device through any channel controlled by the developer. No external servers are involved in storing health records.

☁️ iCloud Sync — Apple CloudKit

If you are signed into an Apple ID with iCloud enabled, your health records may be synchronized across your devices via Apple CloudKit. This synchronization is:

  • End-to-end encrypted and managed entirely by Apple
  • Governed by Apple's Privacy Policy
  • Inaccessible to the developer at all times

You can disable iCloud synchronization at any time from your device's Settings app.

🔐 App Preferences & Local Storage

The App uses local storage (such as SharedPreferences) to store basic operational states and user preferences. This data is stored strictly on your device and includes:

  • Purchase Status: To verify your Pro version status and enable premium features.
  • Consent Records: To remember that you have reviewed and accepted our Privacy Policy and Terms of Use.
  • App Settings: Such as language preferences or display options.

Data Integrity: These preferences do not contain any health-related data (e.g., blood pressure readings) or personally identifiable information (PII).

5Third-Party Services

My BP Diary integrates only the following Apple-provided services, each governed by Apple's Privacy Policy:

🛒 Apple App Store In-App Purchase (IAP)

Purchases of the Pro version upgrade and voluntary support contributions ("Buy Me a Coffee") are processed exclusively through Apple's App Store payment infrastructure. The developer receives no credit card numbers, payment account details, or any financial information from these transactions. All IAP receipts are verified through Apple's servers.

☁️ Apple iCloud / CloudKit

As described in Section 4, CloudKit synchronization is an Apple service. The developer does not operate any backend servers and cannot access CloudKit data belonging to any user.

No other third-party SDKs, analytics libraries, advertising networks, or external APIs are integrated into this application.

6Feedback Communications

When you use the in-app feedback feature to send an email, your email address and the content of your message will be used solely to respond to your inquiry and to improve the application. This information is not shared with any third party.

This communication occurs entirely through your personal email client on your device. No data is sent to or stored on developer-operated servers.

7Children's Privacy

My BP Diary is not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. Since the application does not collect any personal data from any user, children's information is likewise never collected, stored, or transmitted.

If you believe a child has provided personal information through feedback communications, please contact us immediately at the address below so we can take appropriate action.

8Your Rights & Choices

Because all health data is stored locally on your device, you retain full control at all times:

  • Delete individual records — within the application at any time
  • Delete all data — instantly via the app settings, or by uninstalling the application from your device
  • Disable iCloud sync — via iOS Settings > [Your Name] > iCloud > Apps Using iCloud
  • Request refunds — through Apple's standard IAP refund process at reportaproblem.apple.com

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), California, or other jurisdictions with applicable data protection laws, please note that since we do not collect personal data, data subject rights such as access, rectification, and erasure are exercised directly through your device's operating system controls.

9Data Security

The security of your health data is of paramount importance. Because we do not transmit or store your data on external servers, the primary security layer is your device's own operating system, which includes:

  • iOS device encryption (AES-256) when your device is locked
  • Apple's Secure Enclave for biometric authentication
  • Apple CloudKit's end-to-end encryption for synced data

We strongly encourage you to use a strong passcode and enable Face ID/Touch ID on your device to protect access to your health information.

10Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be reflected by updating the "Effective Date" at the top of this page. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.

Your continued use of My BP Diary after any modifications to this Privacy Policy constitutes your acceptance of the updated terms.
